Tips & Tricks

Tips

~ Take an orange stick, or toothpick, and trace your cuticles after each dip to remove any powder from the area.

~ Take a lint free towel and wipe off the brushes after it touches the powder on your nail. This will prevent any contamination of the liquids.

~ Apply cuticle oil to your nails daily to ensuring they stay moisturized.

~ Make nail swatches of each color you have to make picking colors easier.

~ When using a chunkier glitter, put a layer of clear powder on top to help with buffing it smooth

~ Using a glass cuticler pusher works great to get rid of the invisible cuticle on your nails

~ Use a workspace that you don't mind getting messed up if you accidently spill anything

~ Using any sort of silicone mat is also a good idea

~ Start with dipping your dominant hand to ensure you take the extra time to carefully complete it

Tricks

~ You can fix a broken nail by putting a coffee filter over the broken part and continue dipping as usual.

~ Coffee filters can also be used to add length to your nails instead of regular nail tips.

~ If you can't find an exact shade of dip powder to match your favorite polish color, you can encapsulate the polish in the clear powder.

~ Similarly, using nail strips like Color Street, can be encapsulated in clear powder.

~ Acrylic paint pens can be used to draw nail designs on top of the powder, encapsulate it in clear once it dries.

~ If you want to recreate a gradient manicure but dont have enough colors, taking 2 and mixing equal parts of each gives you the perfect color in between them

~ Using a peel base on your nails allows for easy removal without soaking in acetone.

~ Shape your nail before you dip to minimize the amount of filing you need to during Step 5 of the application process.
